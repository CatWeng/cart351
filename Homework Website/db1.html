The letter I chose for this assignment is the letter “C”. I chose it because it’s the first letter of my name so it’s a very familiar letter to me. I like how it has such a simple shape that you can find it in almost anything- the arch of a doorway, the crescent moon, etc.
1. The stylized 5 on a fire extinguisher, reversed. (Quai Alexandra)
2. Canadian National Railway Building logo (935 Rue de la Gauchetière O)
3. A set of handcuffs hanging from my friend’s costume. (École de technologie supérieure)
4. A cement seat by the 715 bus stop. (Pointe-à-Callière Museum)
5. Graffiti on a light post box. (Rue du Port/de la Commune St W)
6. The Canadiens Logo on a poster. (Bell Center)

1.3

For the manipulation of my letter, none of the examples I found was something I could realistically take and manipulate so I created my own. This was done by dropping nail polish into water to create a mosaic of varied colours. Each nail polish interacted with each other and the water differently to create a vivid mix of hues.

1.4



1. For this treatment I keep the original form of the poem but overlapped the text with itself several times. I subtracted and adding until the text had a fragmented but still readable appearance. Inspired by the sense of despair in the poem.
2. For this treatment, I found an illustration (artwork by Kuvshinov Illya) which fit the feeling I got from the poem. I highlighted the word ‘poem’ each time it appeared and then played around with the text until I liked the ‘overwhelming’ look of the image.



3. For the last treatment, I wanted to express the building frustration felt by the woman in the poem and her calm conclusion. For this I used the Zalgo text generator, a generator which stacks characters onto words, to create a feeling of insanity.
